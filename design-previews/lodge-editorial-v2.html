About the Association

Walleye, Waterfowl & Community Since 1982

Walleye and waterfowl are two things that come to mind when outdoorsmen around Minnesota hear the words Lac Qui Parle Lake. The lake has a long tradition of being one of the best walleye fisheries in the state.

Since its inception, the Association has worked in conjunction with the Minnesota DNR, providing funding for walleye stocking programs, fishing piers, boat ramps and rip rap projects on the erodible shorelines.

Walleye Stocking Program

Helping stock 25,000 walleyes in Lac Qui Parle Lake

The LQP Lake Association funds walleye stocking in partnership with the Minnesota DNR. Young-of-the-year fish measuring 7–9 inches are stocked each fall with the bottom left pectoral fin clipped for tracking.

If you catch a walleye with a clipped fin, it was stocked through our program. We'd love to hear about it — email us at minnkotatackle@yahoo.com.

Membership dues are $10 per year. Every dollar goes directly toward lake improvement and conservation projects. Forms are available on the membership page.
